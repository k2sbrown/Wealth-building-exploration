Appreciating assets = ownership of any of: retirement (THRIFT, IRAKH), business equity, future pension, securities (mutual funds, savings bonds, stocks, bonds), other real property, other financial assets, cash-value life insurance, or primary residence. HH without appreciating assets = households owning none of the nine categories above; percent shown is share of that racial group's total households. % home is only appreciating asset = share of all households (including non-owners) for whom a primary residence is their sole appreciating holding — a measure of wealth concentration risk. Mean # of asset types counts how many of the nine categories a household holds, among households owning at least one (unconditional means: White 2.49, Black 1.54, Hispanic 1.26, Other 2.09, All 2.22). Median value is the summed dollar value of those holdings among owners. Weighted to the ~131.3M household universe (WGT summed across all five implicates). Source: Federal Reserve, Survey of Consumer Finances 2022.
